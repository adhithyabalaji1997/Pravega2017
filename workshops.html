Game Development using Virtual Reality

The virtual reality wave is coming! Build your first VR game for Google Cardboard and begin creating VR experiences with our 3D Games Development Workshop with Virtual Reality - the only VR workshop of its kind!
A workshop creatively designed for gaming enthusiasts, it provides an opportunity for gamers to create and develop their own Virtual 3D games. Right from scratch, the workshop indulges its participants to learn how to develop 3D objects, create new models, add blue prints, texture and shape animated objects, etc. This entire process is curated using Maya Software and the developed 3D game is debugged into Virtual game using Worldviz Wizard toolkit. This two day workshop is concluded by installing these games into smartphones and playing thereafter.

Internet of Things

IoT is the most buzzing search on Google .It links smart objects to the Internet. It can enable an exchange of data never available before, and bring users information in a more secure way. Cisco estimates that IoT will consist of 50 billion devices connected to the Internet by 2020.
Gain deeper insight with analytics using our IoT System to enhance productivity, create new business models, and generate new revenue streams. An IoT application platform is a solution that lives in the cloud, meaning it exists virtually somewhere on the Internet. IoT application platforms thrive on being managed, maintained and accessible from virtually anywhere. Cloud-based, connected and driven by data, IoT application platforms provide users a means to implement business use cases otherwise difficult to achieve, such as predictive maintenance, pay-per-use and analytics. Real-time data translates to better business asset utilization and increased productivity. Services are available to interact with these 'smart objects' over the Internet, query and change their state and any information associated with them, taking into account security and privacy issues.
The participants receive a kit in groups of 4 or 5, mutually formed on site. In addition, participants will also receive individual ISO certifications.

Touch and Augmented Reality

We all have five senses; how sad that our connection to computers is “sensory deprived and physically limited,” as Nicholas Negroponte (a founder of MIT’s Media Lab) so aptly phrases it. Visual displays are gradually improving, but our sense of touch is limited for most of the time to the feel of the keyboard and mouse. What will our world be like in 2020? Digital technologies will continue to proliferate, enabling ever more ways of changing how we live. But will such developments improve the quality of life, empower us, and make us feel safer, happier and more connected? Or will living with technology make it more tiresome, frustrating, angst ridden, and security-driven? What will it mean to be human when everything we do is supported or augmented by technology? Why not break away, and wander in smart environments covered in living displays, or carry a system with you as an extension of your senses, augmenting vision? Let us blur the line between real and virtual world. In this 2-day workshop we will explore the opportunities for surface and gesture computing with help of some live demonstration of surface + gesture computing where we will control our PC only with waving hands in air.
Each student will get an ISO certificate and a CD of software and study material. So do come and forget the era of computing with keyboard and mouse and step into a new era of computing where you can control your virtual world with your gestures!
